Introduction

The Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) Census of Population and Housing (the Census) provides summaries by area across many key demographic variables. However, areas enumerated in the Census are defined by the ABS and

  • Are adjusted from year to year, precluding simple longitudinal assessment; and
  • Are not defined at non-ABS area boundaries, such as Local Health District

With the 2021 Census, the ABS has released specific time series Census datasets to allow for longitudinal comparisons in Census variables across consistent geographic boundaries. This allows for direct and valid comparisons to be made across Census variables for a given area over time, without need for conversion or aggregation.

The Public Health Research Analytics and Methods for Evidence unit (PHRAME) has launched an online platform for the visualisation of this time series Census data from 2011, 2016 and 2021, covering three broad topics -

  • Demographic Variables, such as population size, income and age
  • Broad indicators of Cultural and Linguistic Diversity, such as language spoken at home and country of birth
  • ABS indexes of socioeconomic status
  • Self-reported long-term health conditions, included in 2021 for the first time

Specific variables were selected using a combination of relevance to clinical and non-clinical services within SLHD and those that have been reported in previous Census summaries, among those that were available across time. All variables with the exception of the newly included self-reported long-term health conditions are reported across the three most recent censuses (specifically 2011, 2016 and 2021).

Method - Counts and medians

Data Sources

All data was sourced from the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) Census of Population of Housing Application Programming Interface (API). Specifically, Usual resident population (URPs), reflecting the count on Census night by area of usual residence; Median personal weekly income, calculated using the household income census variable, and median age, in whole numbers.

Statistical method

URPs, median ages and median incomes were all available calculated at the SA2 level from the API. No additional summary or calculation was conducted on these estimates. In order to obtain overall Local Health District estimates for populations, URPs were simply summed across all SA2s within a given LHD. In order to obtain overall Local Health District estimates for medians, population-weighted medians were taken across all SA2s within a given LHD.

As estimates were either summed or population weighted, all SA2s were included regardless of population. This method differs slightly from other indicators, where SA2s with populations below the 5th percentile were excluded. However, due to the method of population weighting, any SA2s with low populations are given relatively low weight and hence contribute very little to the overall median.

Limitations

As median personal weekly incomes were not adjusted for inflation, they should only be used for comparison within years, and never between years, without additional adjustment.

All data reported in the census are self-reported, with no validation or cross reference to registry and/or taxation data. The validity of self-reported estimates of income may be subject to bias, and this should be taken into account when interpreting these estimates. Further validation is required before these estimates can be taken at face value.

Usual resident populations were used to standardise the estimates in order to match the numerator. This is unlikely to introduce substantial bias to the estimates, unless certain estimates are higher or lower in populations known be undercounted.
